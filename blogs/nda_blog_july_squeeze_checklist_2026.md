# The July Financial Squeeze: A 5-Point Checklist for South African Households

Electricity, petrol and interest rates have all gone up within weeks of each other. Before it turns into missed payments and stress you can't shake, take five minutes to run through this checklist. No judgement — just five honest questions.

---

### ☐ 1. Recalculate your electricity bill with the increase in mind

Municipal tariffs rose by an average of 9.01% from 1 July 2026, on top of an 8.76% increase municipalities absorbed from Eskom back in April. Your own increase depends on where you live, so check your latest bill rather than guessing.

### ☐ 2. Check your bond or vehicle repayment against the new prime rate

Prime moved from 10.25% to 10.5% after the SARB's 28 May 2026 rate hike. If your home loan or vehicle finance is linked to prime (most are), your repayment has already changed — confirm the new amount with your bank or on your statement.

### ☐ 3. Check what June's petrol spike already cost you

Petrol hit a record R28.06 a litre from 3 June 2026, before easing to R26.10 a litre in the July adjustment. The relief at the pump is real — but two months at record prices already fed through to transport costs, groceries and other knock-on expenses. Check whether your budget has actually recovered, or just adjusted to a "new normal" that's still higher than before June.

### ☐ 4. List every credit agreement and its monthly minimum

Bond, car finance, credit cards, store accounts, personal loans — write down every single one with its current minimum repayment. Most people are surprised by the total once it's all in one place.

### ☐ 5. Compare your total debt repayments to your take-home pay

If everything you owe each month adds up to more than roughly a third to 40% of what you actually take home, that's a signal worth acting on now — not once you've already missed a payment.
