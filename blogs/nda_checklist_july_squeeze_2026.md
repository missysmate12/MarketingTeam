**Subject:** Three cost shocks landed at once. Here's your 5-minute checklist.

Hi there,

If July has felt tighter than usual, you're not imagining it — and you're not alone. In the space of a few weeks, South African households have been hit with **three cost shocks at once**: electricity tariffs went up, petrol is at its highest in years, and loan repayments have climbed with the new prime rate. On their own, any one of these is manageable. Landing together, they can quietly tip a coping budget into a struggling one.

Before you panic, run through this quick checklist. It takes about five minutes and gives you an honest picture of where you actually stand.

### Your July Squeeze Checklist

**1. Recalculate your electricity bill.**
Municipal tariffs rose by around 9% from 1 July 2026. Pull out your last bill and work out what that increase actually adds to your monthly spend in rand.

**2. Check your bond or vehicle repayment against the new prime rate.**
Prime is now 10.5%. If your bond or car finance is linked to prime, your repayment has likely gone up too — check your latest statement or online banking app to confirm the new amount.

**3. Audit your monthly petrol spend.**
With petrol around R28 a litre, work out what your usual monthly fill-ups now cost compared to a few months ago. Small trips add up faster than they used to.

**4. List every credit agreement and its monthly minimum.**
Credit cards, store accounts, personal loans, vehicle finance, your bond — write down every single one, with its minimum monthly payment, in one place. Seeing it all together is often the most useful (if uncomfortable) step.

**5. Add it all up. If your total debt repayments are more than 40% of your take-home pay, it's time to talk to a debt counsellor.**
This isn't a hard scientific line, but it's a widely used rule of thumb: once debt repayments creep past roughly 40% of what you actually take home, there's usually very little room left for essentials — and even less for the unexpected.

### So, how did you score?

If you got through that list without a tight chest, well done — keep an eye on it as rates and tariffs shift. If you landed on point 5 with an uncomfortable number, that's not a failure. It's useful information, and it's exactly the moment when getting help makes the biggest difference.
