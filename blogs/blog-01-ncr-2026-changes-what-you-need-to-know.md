If you've been sitting on the fence about debt review, 2026 might just be the year you take the leap — because the system has never been more consumer-friendly.

The National Credit Regulator (NCR) has rolled out a series of important regulatory updates this year that are specifically designed to protect over-indebted South Africans. Whether you're already under debt review or are considering it, here's what you need to know.

## Stronger Protection Against Reckless Lending

One of the most significant 2026 changes is that credit providers must now apply far stricter affordability assessments before granting credit. That means more thorough income verification, clearer expense categorisation, and detailed record-keeping.

For consumers, this is a game-changer. The days of being approved for a loan you simply couldn't afford are being curtailed at the point of origination — not after you're already drowning in repayments.

At NDA, we've always said: the best debt is the debt you never have to review. These new rules take a big step toward preventing over-indebtedness before it starts.

## More Transparent Fees — No Nasty Surprises

The 2026 updates place a sharp focus on fee transparency. Debt counsellors must now provide you with a full, itemised cost breakdown *before* the process begins. This includes after-care fees and any charges from Payment Distribution Agencies (PDAs).

You must acknowledge these costs in writing before anything proceeds. This protects you from unexpected expenses further down the line and reinforces exactly what a legitimate, reputable debt counsellor should be doing — being completely upfront with you.

At NDA, we've always operated this way. These regulatory changes simply make it the law.

## The NCR Is Watching More Closely Than Ever

Compliance monitoring has been significantly ramped up in 2026. The NCR is conducting more audits and moving faster against debt counsellors who miss deadlines, act unethically, or cut corners.

This is excellent news for consumers. It means:

- Unscrupulous operators face faster consequences
- Your legal protection under debt review is more robustly enforced
- Credit providers who ignore debt review notices or continue collecting unlawfully will face heightened scrutiny

If a credit provider is harassing you while you're legally under debt review, the reinforced enforcement mechanisms mean the NCR can act more swiftly on your complaint.

## Digital Improvements Mean Faster Service

The NCR has been pushing hard for digital integration between debt counsellors, credit providers, Payment Distribution Agencies, and the courts. Electronic document submission and centralised tracking are becoming standard.

This means shorter waiting times, less paperwork getting lost, and a smoother experience from application to clearance certificate. While the NCR's Debt Help System (DHS) experienced downtime earlier in 2026 (now fully restored as of 31 March 2026), the broader digital push is making the system faster and more transparent.

## What This Means If You're Considering Debt Review

The 2026 landscape makes debt review a stronger, safer safety net than it has ever been. But it also means you need to enter the process correctly — with a registered, compliant debt counsellor who follows all the updated guidelines.

Here's what to look for:

1. **NCR registration** — verify your counsellor's credentials on the NCR's official website.
2. **Full fee disclosure upfront** — you should never be surprised by costs.
3. **Clear written explanation** of your rights, timelines, and legal obligations.
4. **Regular communication** and monthly statements throughout the process.
